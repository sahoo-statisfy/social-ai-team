## What Statisfy Does

AI agents that automate customer success work. The platform generates QBR decks in 45 seconds, identifies at-risk accounts, drafts personalized emails, and handles meeting prep/follow-ups.

**Product surface:**
- **Stella AI** — chat interface
- **Predict** — health scores with explainable AI
- **Generate** — document creation (QBRs, briefs, emails)
- **Automate** — workflow execution (next-best-action)
- **Statisfy NoteTaker** — meeting capture

**100+ pre-built agents. Go Live in 2–3 weeks.**

## Target Audience (ICP)

- **Roles (primary):** Customer Success leaders (VPs, Directors, Heads of CS), CSMs, RevOps leaders
- **Roles (secondary):** Sellers / AEs at companies with strong CS motion, COOs/CCOs at scale-stage SaaS
- **Company size:** Scale-stage and Enterprise SaaS (Series B+ through public)
- **Industry:** SaaS / software primarily. Tech-forward services adjacent.
- **What they care about:** GRR / NRR improvement, CSM productivity, reducing manual prep time, getting ahead of churn, defending the renewal, scaling CS without scaling headcount

**Real customer language to mirror:** "Our GRR is up 2%+ this year," "saved 310+ hours," "8–10 hours back per CSM weekly," "we can finally see at-risk accounts before the QBR."

## Positioning & Differentiators

1. **Agents that do the work — not just suggest it.** Statisfy's main contrast vs. "AI copilots" and "AI summaries." The promise is *execution*, not assistance.
2. **45 seconds to generate slides, not 4 hours.** Speed claim, tied to a real artifact (QBR deck).
3. **100+ pre-built agents. Go Live in 2–3 weeks.** Time-to-value is short — they own this against custom-build alternatives.
4. **Explainable health scores.** Predict's explainability is a differentiator against black-box health scoring.
5. **Enterprise-ready compliance.** SOC 2, GDPR, ISO 27001, HIPAA — table stakes for enterprise CS buyers.

## Voice & Tone

**Three-word voice:** Direct. Outcome-focused. Confident.

**Voice rules (always apply):**
- **Lead with the outcome, then the mechanism.** "GRR up 2%" before "explainable health scores."
- **Specific over general.** Numbers, named products, named customers (with permission). Never "drives results" — always "drives [specific result, with number]."
- **Action verbs.** "Saved," "delivered," "unlocked," "generated," "automated." Not "helps with," "enables," "supports."
- **Confident, not hyped.** No "revolutionary," "game-changer," "transformative," "next-gen." The product is strong enough that the copy doesn't need to oversell.
- **Talk to the CS leader.** This person knows the work. Don't explain what a QBR is. Don't define NRR. Trust the reader.
- **First-person on LinkedIn for thought leadership.** Team members posting in their own voice outperform corporate "we."

**Banned phrases (AI filler / SaaS clichés):**
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "Let's dive in"
- "Game-changer" / "revolutionary" / "transformative" / "paradigm shift"
- "Unlock the power of"
- "It's no secret that"
- "I'm excited to share"
- "At the end of the day"
- "This is a reminder that"
- "Supercharge" / "10x" / "next-gen"
- "Customer-centric" (everyone says this — say what you actually do)
- Generic "AI is changing everything" openers

**Required terminology:**
- **"AI agents"** (not "AI copilots," not "AI assistants" — *agents that do the work*)
- **Product names capitalised:** Stella AI, Predict, Generate, Automate, Statisfy NoteTaker
- **"Next-best-action"** (hyphenated)
- **"Health scores"** with **"explainable AI"** when discussing Predict
- **"Go Live"** (their phrase for time-to-value)

## Content Pillars (default mix)

| Pillar | Ratio | What it looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **1. AI Agents at Work** | 25% | Concrete examples of agents doing CS work. "How Stella turns a 4-hour QBR into 45 seconds." Screenshots, demos, before/after. |
| **2. The Modern CS Org** | 25% | Opinion/thought leadership on how CS teams should operate in the AI era. POV pieces. Contrarian takes. |
| **3. Customer Outcomes** | 20% | Real metrics from named customers (with permission): Observe.ai, Sendoso, Cerby, Gremlin, Mixpanel, Illumio, Eightfold.ai, Skyflow, Milestone. Quoted GRR/NRR/hours-saved numbers. |
| **4. Frameworks & Playbooks** | 15% | Educational. Health scoring frameworks, QBR structures, renewal motion playbooks, RevOps templates. High-save content. |
| **5. Product & Platform** | 10% | Stella AI / Predict / Generate / Automate / NoteTaker updates. Integration spotlights (Salesforce, HubSpot, Zendesk). |
| **6. Industry Commentary** | 5% | React to CS / RevOps / AI news, Pulse data, Gainsight earnings, etc. Reactive content. |

**Adjustment rules:**
- If goal = lead gen / demo bookings → +5% Customer Outcomes, +5% Product, –10% Industry Commentary
- If goal = thought leadership / authority → +10% The Modern CS Org, –5% Product, –5% Industry Commentary
- If goal = product launch / feature push → +15% Product, –5% from three other pillars
- If goal = community / engagement → +10% The Modern CS Org (contrarian), +5% Industry Commentary, –10% Product, –5% Customer Outcomes

## Platform Strategy

**Primary:** LinkedIn — this is where CS leaders, RevOps, and enterprise SaaS buyers live. Default frequency: 4–5x/week.

**Secondary:** X / Twitter — for industry commentary, contrarian takes, and CS-Twitter community. Default frequency: 3x/week.

**Tertiary / opportunistic:** YouTube (long-form product demos), podcast appearances.

**Generally skip for Statisfy:** Instagram, Threads, TikTok, Facebook. The audience is not meaningfully present on these platforms in a way that justifies the production overhead. If a campaign demands one of them, treat it as a one-off, not a regular pillar. Skills for these platforms (`/caption-writer`, `/threads-writer`) are kept in this workspace but are deprioritised — only run them when a specific reason exists.

## Visual Identity

### Colour palette

**Brand purple (primary accent):**
| Token | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Primary | `#8d57f7` | Headline numbers, CTAs, links, accent strokes, key UI highlights |
| Primary light | `#a77bfa` | Hover states, soft highlights, gradient pairs with Primary |
| Primary dark | `#7040d4` | Pressed states, deeper accent on light surfaces |

**Navy (dark surfaces — Statisfy's default background register):**
| Token | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Navy 950 | `#02030a` | Deepest background — full-bleed hero panels, dark-mode primary surface |
| Navy 900 | `#080b1c` | Default dark surface |
| Navy 800 | `#0c1229` | Cards / containers on dark surfaces |
| Navy 700 | `#131b3d` | Elevated cards / dividers on dark surfaces |

**Neutrals:**
| Token | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Dark text | `#1a1a1a` | Body text on light surfaces |
| Gray text | `#5f6368` | Secondary text, captions, attributions |

**Light surfaces (purple-tinted neutrals for section beds):**
| Token | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Surface 50 | `#f8f9fa` | Default light page surface |
| Surface 100 | `#f5f0ff` | Soft purple-tinted section bed |
| Surface 200 | `#ede5ff` | Stronger purple-tinted callouts and highlighted blocks |

### Typography

- **Heading:** **Poppins** — weight **600** (semi-bold), fallback `sans-serif`
- **Body:** **Inter** — fallback `sans-serif`
- **Case:** sentence case for headlines and body. Title Case for product names (Stella AI, Predict, Generate, Automate, Statisfy NoteTaker).
- **Numerals:** numerals always — "45 seconds," "2%+ GRR," "100+ agents."

### Logo

White Statisfy wordmark on dark navy surfaces. Dark text wordmark (`#1a1a1a`) on light surfaces.

### Photography style

Product screenshots, headshots of customer-quote subjects, clean UI captures. **Not** lifestyle imagery, not stock photos, not abstract "AI" glow-orb art.

### Infographic style

Data-forward. Stat cards, process diagrams (3-step / 5-step), framework diagrams, quote graphics with attribution to titled customer (e.g. "VP of CS at Observe.ai").

**Default infographic palette:**
- Background: `#080b1c` (Navy 900) or `#02030a` (Navy 950) for full-bleed depth
- Container / card: `#0c1229` (Navy 800)
- Primary text: white / off-white
- Secondary text: gray (`#5f6368` on light, off-white on dark)
- **Accent / hero number / highlight:** `#8d57f7` (Primary purple) — this is the colour that does the visual work
- Optional gradient: `#8d57f7` → `#7040d4` for hero numbers or CTA strokes

**Light-surface infographic variant** (rarer — for blog cross-posts or carousel slides that need contrast against a dark feed):
- Background: `#f5f0ff` or `#ede5ff` (purple-tinted)
- Primary text: `#1a1a1a`
- Accent: `#8d57f7`

**Visual do/don't:**
- DO: keep one idea per image
- DO: use a real screenshot of Stella, Predict, Generate, etc. when the post is product-focused
- DO: pair customer quotes with the customer's company logo (with permission)
- DON'T: use stock photos of people pointing at laptops
- DON'T: use generic "AI" visual tropes (glow orbs, circuit-board overlays, blue gradient brains)
- DON'T: invent UI screenshots — only use real product captures supplied by the design team

## Do / Don't (content)

### DO
- Lead with the specific outcome or number, then explain the mechanism
- Quote real customer titles ("VP of CS at [Company]")
- Use "AI agents" — not "AI assistants" / "AI copilots"
- Be specific: "QBR in 45 seconds" not "QBRs faster"
- Take a stance on how CS should operate in the AI era — Statisfy has a point of view
- Reference specific products by name (Stella, Predict, Generate, Automate, NoteTaker)
- Mention integrations by name (Salesforce, HubSpot, Zendesk, Gainsight) when relevant
- Use **Poppins 600** for headlines on visuals, **Inter** for body text
- Use **`#8d57f7`** as the accent in every Statisfy social visual — it's the colour that makes the asset recognisably Statisfy

### DON'T
- Use AI-hype filler (banned phrases above)
- Promise outcomes you can't back up with a real customer number
- Talk about "the future of CS" abstractly — name what changes and by how much
- Use lifestyle photography or stock imagery
- Write content that could be posted by any AI vendor — every post must be specifically *Statisfy*
- Position against named competitors by name in social — speak to the category, not the rival
- Use hashtag stuffing — LinkedIn 3–5 max, X zero

## Compliance & Claims

Statisfy operates in enterprise SaaS. Claims have weight. Before publishing:

- **Customer metrics:** never invent a number. If a number isn't in a public case study, press release, or signed-off marketing asset, don't post it.
- **Compliance language:** SOC 2 Type II, GDPR, ISO 27001, HIPAA — these can be referenced. Do not extend the list.
- **Competitor comparisons:** speak to the category, not the named rival ("most AI summaries stop at suggestion — Statisfy executes" is fine; "[Competitor X] only suggests" is not).
- **Product capability claims:** must match what the product actually does today, not the roadmap. If unsure, route to the product marketing lead.
