# Hook Library — Statisfy

Hook formulas tuned for Statisfy's voice — direct, outcome-focused, peer-to-peer with CS / RevOps leaders. Use as a starting point. Adapt to the specific post concept.

> **Voice reminders:**
> - Lead with the outcome and the number where you have one
> - "AI agents" — never "AI copilots" or "AI assistants"
> - Banned: "revolutionary," "game-changer," "transformative," "supercharge," "10x," "let's dive in," "in today's fast-paced world," "I'm excited to share"

---

## Outcome / Number Hooks (Statisfy default)

Pattern: lead with the result, name the size, then explain.

- "QBR prep used to take our team 4 hours. Stella does it in 45 seconds."
- "Observe.ai's GRR is up 2%+ this year. Here's the operating change behind that number."
- "100+ pre-built agents. Go Live in 2–3 weeks. The 12-month CS tool implementation is over."
- "8–10 hours back per CSM, every week. That's the difference between a tool that *suggests* and an agent that *does*."
- "[Customer name] saved 310+ hours in 90 days. One agent. One workflow."
- "[Specific outcome] — and it happened in [time]. Here's what changed —"

---

## Contrarian / POV Hooks

For The Modern CS Org pillar. State the opposite of received wisdom.

- "Most 'AI for CS' stops at the summary. The summary is where the actual work starts."
- "Your CSMs don't need another copilot. They need agents that *do* the work."
- "If your health scores can't explain themselves, your CSMs won't trust them."
- "CS budgets are flat. NRR targets are up. The math only works if agents do the prep."
- "The QBR isn't a slide problem. It's a data-pull problem."
- "Renewal motions don't fail in the renewal quarter. They fail in the QBR three quarters earlier."
- "[Common CS belief] is wrong. Here's what the data actually shows."

## Customer-Voice Hooks

For Customer Outcomes pillar. Permission required.

- "'Our GRR is up 2%+ this year.' — VP CS, Observe.ai"
- "[Customer name] CCO on the operating change: '[direct quote]'"
- "[Customer name] saved [number] hours in [timeframe]. Here's how their CS team uses Statisfy."
- "A [job title] at [customer] told us [insight]. It changed how we think about [topic]."
- "[Customer] used to run [old motion]. Now they run [new motion]. The result: [number]."

## Industry Commentary Hooks

For Industry Commentary pillar. React to the trigger event.

- "OpenAI just shipped [model / agent feature]. The CS implication isn't what most people are saying."
- "Gainsight just announced [thing]. Here's how to read it from the operator seat —"
- "Conference takeaway after [event]: [specific observation]. Here's what changes for CS leaders next quarter."
- "The category is starting to talk about [topic]. The part they're not saying:"

## Question Hooks

For engagement and reply-driving content. Use sparingly.

- "What's the first CS workflow you'd hand to an agent?"
- "How long does your team spend on QBR prep — be honest?"
- "If your health score paged you tomorrow, would the CSM know what to do?"
- "What's a CS task you secretly know shouldn't be human work anymore?"
- "Which is harder this year: hitting NRR or hitting the bar for net-new logos?"

---

## Story Hooks

For first-person exec voice posts. Specific, low-warmup.

- "Last week I watched a CSM spend 4 hours on a QBR. The customer barely engaged with it."
- "A VP CS told me [specific situation]. It crystallised the thing I've been trying to say about agents for months."
- "Two years ago we built a health score that nobody trusted. Here's what we got wrong —"
- "I stopped doing [old CS practice] in [timeframe]. The result: [outcome]."
- "When [customer] told us [specific thing], we changed [specific thing]. Here's what we learned —"

## Cross-Post Tightening for X (≤280 chars)

When converting a LinkedIn hook to X, drop the warmup, keep the number, end on the contrast.

- LinkedIn: "QBR prep used to take our CS managers 4 hours. Stella does it in 45 seconds — with health-score history, usage data, and the last three QBRs already pulled."
- X: "QBR prep: 4 hours → 45 seconds. Stella pulls health history, usage data, and prior QBRs. The CSM walks in ready, not catching up."

---

## Hook Checklist (Statisfy)

Before finalising any hook, check:

- [ ] Does it lead with the outcome / number / contrarian take?
- [ ] Does it work as a standalone line, no context needed?
- [ ] Would a Director of CS stop scrolling for this on a busy LinkedIn feed?
- [ ] Does it match Statisfy's voice — direct, confident, peer-to-peer?
- [ ] Is it free of banned phrases (revolutionary, game-changer, supercharge, etc.)?
- [ ] If it names a customer or metric, is permission confirmed in `context/customer-roster.md`?
- [ ] Does "AI agents" appear if relevant — never "AI copilots / assistants"?
