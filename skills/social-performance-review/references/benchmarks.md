# Social Benchmarks — Statisfy (B2B SaaS)

Reference benchmarks for evaluating Statisfy's organic social performance. **Do not compare Statisfy to consumer / SMB benchmarks** — B2B SaaS engagement rates run materially lower than lifestyle accounts and compensate in click-through and pipeline value.

Use these as context, not as targets. The goal is **consistent improvement month-over-month** plus **demand-gen impact** (clicks to statisfy.com, demos sourced from social) — not chasing an industry-average number.

---

## LinkedIn — Statisfy's Primary Channel

### Company Page

**Engagement rate** = (reactions + comments + reposts + clicks) ÷ impressions × 100

| Rating | B2B SaaS company page ER |
|---|---|
| Below average | < 1.5% |
| Average | 1.5–3% |
| Good | 3–5% |
| Excellent | 5%+ |

**Note:** LinkedIn personal profiles typically see 2–3× higher engagement than company pages. Track them separately. Aggregate company-page ER and exec-personal ER as two distinct numbers in the review.

### Personal Profile (named Statisfy execs)

| Rating | Exec personal profile ER |
|---|---|
| Below average | < 3% |
| Average | 3–6% |
| Good | 6–10% |
| Excellent | 10%+ |

**Click-through rate** — clicks to statisfy.com (book-a-demo or product page) ÷ impressions × 100

| Rating | LinkedIn CTR (B2B SaaS) |
|---|---|
| Below average | < 0.3% |
| Average | 0.3–0.8% |
| Good | 0.8–1.5% |
| Excellent | 1.5%+ |

**Note:** CTR is *the* B2B SaaS demand-gen metric on LinkedIn. Anchor the review here when attribution is available. A 1% CTR on a 100K-impression month is 1,000 clicks to statisfy.com — far more meaningful than ER alone.

### Follower growth (monthly)

| Rating | Monthly growth |
|---|---|
| Flat | 0–0.5% |
| Average | 0.5–2% |
| Good | 2–4% |
| Strong | 4%+ |

### Posting frequency (recommended for Statisfy)

| Channel | Recommended frequency |
|---|---|
| Company page | 4–5×/week |
| Exec personals (per active exec) | 2–4×/week |

## Demand-Gen Metrics (B2B SaaS — most important)

These are the metrics that matter to Statisfy more than ER:

| Metric | How to measure | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Clicks to statisfy.com from social** | UTM-tagged links + LinkedIn / X analytics click count | Top-of-funnel demand signal |
| **Demos sourced from social** | Marketing automation source attribution (first-touch or last-touch) | Direct pipeline tie-back |
| **MQLs sourced from social** | Form-fill source = social | Mid-funnel |
| **Cost per click vs cost per demo** | Demand-gen team's attribution model | Used for paid/organic blended view |

When attribution is available, **anchor the monthly review here** rather than ER.

## Format Benchmarks (LinkedIn — B2B SaaS)

Relative performance per format (index: single image post = 100):

| Format | Impressions | Reactions | Comments | Reposts | Click-through |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Text post (no image) | 90–110 | 100 | 110–140 | 90–120 | 80–100 |
| Single image | 100 | 100 | 100 | 100 | 100 |
| Quote graphic / stat card | 110–140 | 130–160 | 100–130 | 130–170 | 110–140 |
| Carousel (PDF) | 130–180 | 150–200 | 140–180 | 200–300 | 80–120 |
| Video (product demo) | 150–250 | 140–180 | 120–160 | 130–170 | 150–250 |

**Interpretation for Statisfy:**
- **Carousels** are LinkedIn's highest-repost format for B2B — push Frameworks & Playbooks and multi-slide Customer Outcomes here
- **Video product demos** are Statisfy's highest CTR format — watching Stella generate a QBR or Predict surface a health score drives demos. Production cost is high but ROI is the clearest in the format mix.
- **Quote graphics** outperform plain text on reach and reposts — drive Customer Outcomes through them
- **Text posts** still win for comments and engagement on opinion / thought leadership — exec voices on The Modern CS Org pillar should default here

## Pillar-Level Benchmarks (Statisfy-specific — build over time)

These do not have industry benchmarks yet — they're pillar-relative comparisons across Statisfy's own months. After the first 3 months of `/social-performance-review` runs, the trailing-3-month average of each pillar becomes its own benchmark.

Track per pillar:
- Avg impressions
- Avg engagement rate
- Avg click-through rate
- Avg demos sourced (when attribution is available)

A pillar that consistently lags the others on click-through is a candidate for ratio reduction. A pillar that consistently leads is a candidate for ratio increase — subject to brand-strategy considerations (you can't have an all-Customer-Outcomes calendar even if it converts best).

## Category Context (B2B SaaS — informational)

For external comparison only. Industry reports (LinkedIn, HubSpot, Sprout Social) typically cite these averages for B2B SaaS:

- LinkedIn company-page ER: 2–3% is typical, 4%+ is top quartile
- LinkedIn CTR to website: 0.5–1% is typical, 1.5%+ is top quartile
- Monthly follower growth: 1–2% is typical, 3%+ is top quartile
- Posts per week on company page: 3–5 is typical for active B2B SaaS pages

Statisfy's category peers (Gainsight, ChurnZero, Catalyst, Vitally, Planhat) — when reviewed via `/social-performance-review` Phase 4 — provide a more relevant benchmark than industry averages.

---

## Using These Benchmarks

1. **First review** — compare Statisfy against these external benchmarks to flag obvious gaps
2. **Subsequent reviews** — Statisfy's own prior month becomes the primary benchmark. External benchmarks become context.
3. **Don't use SMB / consumer benchmarks.** B2B SaaS engagement runs lower; click-through and pipeline value matter more.
4. **Anchor on demand-gen metrics when attribution is available.** Click-through to statisfy.com and demos sourced from social tell the real story.
5. **Never present external benchmarks as targets.** They're context. Statisfy's goal is sustained month-over-month improvement on CTR and demos sourced — not chasing a 5% ER on the company page.
